§ I

What we hold ourselves to.

Three commitments. Boring on purpose. The kind of boring that compounds.

Goal → Strategy → Tactic.

Every campaign decomposes the same way. Nothing happens without a goal that ties to a business outcome you actually have.

Operator-in-the-loop.

The system proposes. You commit, revise, or send it back to its jurisdiction. It does not act without you. That is the whole shape of it.

Evidence over claims.

Every output is traceable to its inputs. No black-box “AI did it.” You can read the whole pipeline, top to bottom, on a Tuesday afternoon.

§ II

What this is not.

Most of what gets sold as marketing software is sold to a different reader. We’d rather say so plainly than imply otherwise.

Anti-positioning
  • Not a growth-hacking tool.
  • Not an AI content firehose.
  • Not a way to “do marketing while you sleep.”
  • Not for businesses that need to triple revenue in six months.

If any of those are what you’re looking for, we genuinely wish you well — there are better tools out there for that job.

§ III

What it actually does.

A season, in plain language. The same shape every time.

You give it your roots — your offer, your proof, your brand voice, your constraints. Bristlecone reads them once and remembers.

It plans a season. It drafts the cones — the campaigns — and shows them to you before anything ships.

You commit, revise, or push back. When you commit, the system executes through real channels. Real email, real LinkedIn, real direct outreach. No mailto-link theater.

Every decision and every outcome is recorded in your rings. Year over year, the picture sharpens. Patience is the substrate.

§ IV

Compound — read literally.

A bristlecone pine grows about an inch per decade. We don’t have a chart. We have a ledger.

Year 01First ring. a season; a base­line; one honest hello
Year 05First compound. five seasons; the pattern visible
Year 10First canopy. a customer base that quietly insists
Year 30A grove. the work outlasts the launch
Year 4,800Still standing. Methuselah, the oldest known specimen
§ V

Who this is for.

Self-selection is a kindness to both of us.

Built for

Businesses with a 10–50 year horizon.

  • Solo operators and small teams.
  • Trades, professional services, craft makers, long-tail SaaS.
  • People who’ve been burned by aggressive growth tools.
  • Comfortable approving a draft before it ships.
  • Patient. Or willing to become patient.
Not built for

Funded startups with hockey-stick KPIs.

  • Anyone who needs results next week.
  • Marketers who want full automation with no oversight.
  • Allergic to reading drafts before they ship.
  • In a hurry to triple revenue this quarter.
  • Looking for the “AI does it all” promise.

If you’re in the second column, we wish you well — and we mean it.
