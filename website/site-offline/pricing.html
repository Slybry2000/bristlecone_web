faq

The questions you should ask.

Answered plainly. If something here surprises you in either direction, write to us.

How does auto-renewal work?

Annual plans renew automatically unless cancelled. We send a reminder email 30 days before each renewal so you can decide before money moves. Cancellation is one button in your account settings — no exit interview, no save offer, no hold.

Which tier is right for me?

If you’re marketing one business, start with Sapling — that’s the design-partner tier and it includes the week-12 commitment. Grove is for teams or operators running multiple brands. Old Growth is for established businesses that want a working session with our team each quarter. If you’re unsure, write — we’d rather you start in the right place than upgrade twice.

What if I outgrow my tier?

Move up at any time, prorated. We don’t lock features behind aggressive caps to push you up; the tiers reflect how teams actually use the product. Sapling will hold a one-person operation for years.

What does “one Season” actually mean?

A Season is a campaign cycle — about a quarter (~12 weeks). It maps to one Goal, one Strategy or two, and the Tactics those produce. Sapling caps you at four Seasons a year (one per quarter). Grove and Old Growth let you run more than one Season at the same time, useful when you’re marketing several brands or audiences.

What does “Heartwood governance” mean in practice?

It’s the approval system: every consequential decision the system proposes gets a commit (you sign off), revise (you redirect), or jurisdiction (you say this isn’t the system’s call). On Old Growth we write custom policies for how your team handles those gates — e.g., who can commit what, what auto-routes to revise, etc.

Is there a free tier?

No. Free tiers tend to compromise the product for the people paying for it. We’d rather charge fairly than build for the freeloader. If pricing is a real barrier, write to us.

Refunds?

Within 30 days of your first annual payment, no questions. After that, a pro-rata refund for whichever Season you didn’t finish. Design-partner seats also include the week-12 commitment: if we miss it, the seat is refunded and the miss is listed at /failed (see Terms for the contractual definition of “closed reply loop”).

EU/UK: what about the 14-day right of withdrawal?

EU/UK customers have a 14-day right to withdraw from a distance-sold digital service. If you ask us to start the season’s work inside that 14-day window, you’re waiving the withdrawal right for what we’ve already done — the 30-day refund still applies.

Cancellation?

A button. No exit interview, no “wait, you qualify for a discount” popup, no holds. Annual plans cancel at term end; monthly plans cancel within the day.

Do you train models on my data?

We do not use your Roots, Cones, or Rings to train Bristlecone’s models, and we contractually require the same of every model provider we route through. The current sub-processors and their data terms are listed at /legal/subprocessors.html.

Can I export my history?

Yes, always. JSON for the structured data, Markdown for the human-readable Cones, PDF for the rings reports. Export is a button, not a support ticket.

What if Bristlecone goes away?

Your data is yours, exportable in formats listed above. We will give 12 months’ notice and a working export endpoint either way. We’d rather wind down honestly than disappear quietly.
